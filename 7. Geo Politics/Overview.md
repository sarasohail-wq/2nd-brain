# Geo Politics: Global Trends & Analysis

## 1. The Rise of "Multi-Alignment"
We are moving away from a strictly bipolar or unipolar world. Countries like **India, Saudi Arabia, and Brazil** are practicing "multi-alignment," refusing to pick sides between major powers and instead building diverse partnerships based on national interest.

## 2. The Silicon Shield & Semiconductor Diplomacy
Semiconductors are the new oil. 
*   **Taiwan (TSMC):** Produces 90% of the world's most advanced chips, creating a "Silicon Shield" that makes its security a global economic necessity.
*   **The Chips Act:** Both the US and EU are investing billions to "re-shore" chip manufacturing to reduce dependency on East Asia.

## 3. The Great Energy Transition
The shift from fossil fuels to renewables is creating new winners and losers.
*   **Critical Minerals:** Countries with large reserves of **Lithium (Chile/Australia)**, **Cobalt (DRC)**, and **Rare Earths (China)** now hold significant geopolitical leverage.
*   **Green Hydrogen:** Emergence of "Hydrogen Hubs" in North Africa and the Middle East as they look to pivot away from oil.

## 4. Regionalization of Trade
Global supply chains are shortening.
*   **Near-shoring:** US companies moving manufacturing to Mexico.
*   **Friend-shoring:** Moving manufacturing to countries with shared values to minimize political risk.
