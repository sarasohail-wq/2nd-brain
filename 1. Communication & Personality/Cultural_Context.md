# Cross-Cultural Communication: High vs. Low Context

## 1. High-Context Cultures
(e.g., Japan, China, Arab countries, Mediterranean countries)
*   **Implicit Communication:** Much of the message is conveyed through non-verbal cues, status, and the setting.
*   **Relationships First:** Trust is built over time before business is conducted.
*   **Indirectness:** "No" might be expressed as "That might be difficult."

## 2. Low-Context Cultures
(e.g., USA, Germany, Scandinavia, Switzerland)
*   **Explicit Communication:** Information is stated clearly and directly. "Say what you mean."
*   **Task First:** Business can be conducted quickly even without a deep personal relationship.
*   **Directness:** Feedback is often blunt and straightforward.

## 3. Active Listening Techniques
*   **Mirroring:** Repeating the last few words the speaker said to encourage them to continue.
*   **Labeling:** "It sounds like you are feeling..." to validate emotions and gather more info.
*   **Dynamic Silence:** Using pauses to let the other person fill the space with more information.
