# Pacing Analysis

## 1. Current Pacing: 128 WPM
Your speed is **128 words per minute (WPM)**. This is generally considered an ideal "Conversational" pace.
*   **The Good:** At this speed, you are easy to follow. You aren't rushing, which allows the listener to process your information, but you aren't so slow that they might lose interest.
*   **Comparison:** For reference, most professional speakers and audiobook narrators aim for 130–150 WPM. You are on the calmer, more deliberate end of that spectrum.

## 2. Pacing Variation
The graph shows your pace over time.
*   **Consistency:** You stayed mostly within the "Conversational" shaded blue zone (the 130-160 range).
*   **Dips and Peaks:** There are some moments where your pace dipped slightly below 100 WPM and peaked just around 150 WPM. This is actually a **positive** sign of "Dynamic Speech." Speaking at a perfectly flat speed is monotonous; natural variation keeps a listener engaged.

## Feedback & Recommendations:
1.  **Maintain the Baseline:** Your average of 128 is a great "anchor." Use this for delivering key facts or when you want to sound authoritative and calm.
2.  **Strategic Speed-Up:** When you are sharing an exciting idea or a personal anecdote, it's okay to let that variation peak higher (towards 150). It signals passion.
3. The Power of the Pause: Those dips in your graph (below 120) are great places for pauses. Ensure those dips represent intentional pauses for emphasis, rather than filler words (um, ah).
