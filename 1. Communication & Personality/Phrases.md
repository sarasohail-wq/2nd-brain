# Communication & Pitching Phrases

A quick-reference playbook for consultative selling, cross-cultural communication, and professional engagement.

## 1. The "Check-In" Rule
*Use these every 3-4 minutes to verify alignment and break up monologues.*

*   "I want to pause there—how does that alignment with what you've seen in your department?"
*   "Before I move on, does that specific part of the workflow match your current experience?"

## 2. The Bridge Questions
*Use these to transition from technical expertise back to the client's business reality.*

### The Bottleneck Bridge
*   "Based on your current bottlenecks, where do you see the most potential for this solution?"

### The Perspective Bridge
*   "I’ve shared a lot there—which of these three areas (e.g., Agents, Multimodality, or Reasoning) feels most relevant to your specific challenges?"

### The Roadmap Bridge
*   "How does this change your perspective on your 2026 roadmap?"

## 3. Tactical Empathy & Labels
*Derived from Chris Voss/Never Split the Difference principles.*

*   **Mirroring:** Repeating the last 1-3 critical words the speaker said.
*   **Labeling:** "It sounds like you are feeling..." or "It seems like there is some hesitation regarding..."
*   **Dynamic Silence:** Pausing after a label to let the other person fill the space.

## 4. Cross-Cultural (High-Context) Adjustments
*For cultures where directness can be seen as aggressive.*

*   **Instead of "No":** "That might be difficult" or "We will look into the feasibility of that."
*   **Building Rapport:** "I'd love to understand more about your team's vision before we dive into the numbers."

## 5. Precision & Conciseness
*To maintain the 128 WPM "Conversational" anchor and reduce fillers.*

*   **Direct Starters:** Start with the subject.
    *   *Bad:* "So, what we did was..."
    *   *Good:* "We implemented..."
*   **The Power Pause:** Replace "um," "ah," and "so" with 1-2 seconds of silence.
